Project: Vision-Guided Robotic Automation for LED Manufacturing

I developed a semi-automated robotic system to optimize the manufacturing process for an LED production line. The goal was to reduce manufacturing time by leveraging robotic automation and vision systems. To achieve this, I integrated a Cognex 9000 series vision system with a UR-5 robotic arm, enabling precise hole detection and positioning. The camera was trained to recognize multiple hole locations on the LED components and extract their exact coordinates. These coordinates were then transmitted to the UR-5 robot using Serial Native Robot, TCP/IP, and Ethernet/IP protocols, ensuring seamless communication between the vision system and the robotic controller. Once the hole positions were identified, I implemented URScript and socket programming within UR Polyscope to dynamically control the UR-5 robot‚Äôs motion. This allowed the robot to automatically adjust its movements based on real-time data received from the vision system, significantly improving accuracy and efficiency.

The automation of this process led to a 1-minute reduction in manufacturing time per product for one LED model. By eliminating manual intervention and optimizing robotic movements, this solution enhanced production efficiency, consistency, and repeatability, contributing to overall process improvement in the LED manufacturing workflow.
